NoF 2024 Best Paper Award:
Yinan Qian

The Organizing Committee of the 15th International Conference on Network of the Future (NoF 2024) is pleased to announce that Yinan Qian has been selected to receive the NoF 2024 Best Paper Award for outstanding research in edge-to-cloud traffic steering.

Best Paper Award — NoF 2024

Adaptive Edge-to-Cloud Traffic Steering for Latency-Critical Applications in Next-Generation CDN Architectures

Award Details

Title Adaptive Edge-to-Cloud Traffic Steering for Latency-Critical Applications in Next-Generation CDN Architectures
Author Yinan Qian (Pinterest, Inc., San Francisco, CA, United States)
Affiliation Pinterest, Inc. · Tufts University, Medford, MA
Award Best Paper Award, NoF 2024
Date Conferred 4 October 2024
Published In Proceedings of the 15th NoF, IEEE Xplore
DOI 10.1109/NoF64105.2024

About the Winning Paper

Emerging latency-critical applications such as real-time video processing, online gaming, and autonomous system coordination demand deterministic end-to-end latency guarantees that traditional CDN architectures struggle to provide. This paper introduces a novel adaptive traffic steering framework that dynamically routes requests across edge-to-cloud continuums based on real-time network conditions, application requirements, and edge node capacity.

The proposed system integrates a lightweight latency predictor with a multi-objective optimization engine that balances strict latency constraints against cost and throughput objectives. Evaluations using production traffic traces demonstrate consistent latency reductions of 31% at the p99 tail, 24% improvement in request success rate under load spikes, and 19% reduction in cloud egress costs when compared to conventional anycast and geo-based routing strategies.

About the Author

Yinan Qian is an Infrastructure Engineer at Pinterest, Inc., where she works on CDN strategy, DNS configuration, and edge service architecture using Envoy proxy. She previously served as a Software Development Engineer at Amazon Web Services, contributing to the VPC Reachability Analyzer and Network Access Analyzer teams. She holds an M.S. in Software Engineering from Tufts University. Her research interests include content delivery infrastructure, edge computing, and adaptive routing algorithms for next-generation networks.

Selection Process

The Best Paper Award was selected by the NoF 2024 Technical Program Committee. Papers were evaluated on originality, technical quality, clarity of presentation, and potential impact on future internet research. The NoF 2024 committee recognised two outstanding papers of equal merit. The awards were presented during the closing session on 4 October 2024.

About NoF 2024

The 15th edition of the International Conference on Network of the Future was held in Castelldefels / Barcelona, Spain, from 2 to 4 October 2024. Organised by DNAC, France, the conference featured technical sessions, keynote speeches, and the Best Paper Award ceremony. The proceedings are published in IEEE Xplore.
